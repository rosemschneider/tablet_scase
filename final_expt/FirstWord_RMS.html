A short survey about children's first words (excluding "Mama" and "Dada" or their equivalents).

Please answer for one child at a time!


We consider a "word" to be a consistent use of a particular set of sounds to indicate a consistent meaning. So, for example, if your baby babbled like "babababa" when she was excited, that would would not be a word. But if she used "moomoo" consistently to mean "cow," that would be a good word.
